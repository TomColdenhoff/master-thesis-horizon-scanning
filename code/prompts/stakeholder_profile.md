Monitor all Dutch parliamentary documents for early signals of regulatory change that may affect clients across any sector of Dutch law. Relevant documents include ministerial letters (brieven regering), questions and answers (schriftelijke vragen), and legislative proposals that signal new obligations, prohibitions, competences, or permit requirements — at any stage from research commission to internet consultation to bill submission. Policy domains include but are not limited to: housing and tenancy, employment and labour, environmental regulation, financial services, data protection, public procurement, competition law, and corporate governance. Exclude purely procedural communications, press releases, and documents with no normative content.
